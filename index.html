About Me

Welcome to my webpage! I'm Jenny - a mathematician from Ireland. I am a 3rd year PhD student at the University of Bath, funded by the SAMBA Centre for Doctoral Training. My research focuses on PDE-constrained optimisation applied to radiotherapy trearment planning.

Jenny is a second-year PhD student and an applied mathematician from Shannon Co. Clare. She is a UL alumna and graduated with a BSc. in Mathematics and Physics in 2020. While at UL, she completed her Co-Op at TNO Holst Centre (Eindhoven, the Netherlands) where she worked on laser-printed nanotechnology. She also received a 2018 Science and Engineering research bursary where she conducted a research project in the field of geometrical optics, and also worked in this area for her final year project. Jenny was also involved in the SOPHia project since its inception in 2018, participating in around ten school visits. Jenny is currently pursuing her PhD in Statistical Applied Maths at the University of Bath (UK) through the . Jenny’s research interests lie in applied maths (particularly with applications to medical physics), partial differential equations, and numerical analysis. Her PhD is on the topic of optimising radiotherapy cancer treatments. Recently, Jenny competed in STEM for Britain, a major scientific poster competition organised by and held in UK Parliament, where she won the silver award in the mathematics category. Jenny also was runner-up in the Smith Institute’s annual TakeAIM competition and was awarded the opportunity to present her research at the Science Museum in London. Alongside her PhD, Jenny does science communication and documents her PhD journey on her Instagram page @thejennypowerhour.
